# Mastering Discovery Calls to Close Deals Effectively

By Chris Orlob (Gong Labs research, based on AI analysis of 519,000 recorded discovery calls).

Running an effective discovery call often means the difference between winning and losing a sale. Your discovery call sets the trajectory of the deal. It dictates how you present, what objection handling scenarios you'll run into, and how much negotiation leverage you find yourself with at the end of the sales process.

So, what makes a winning discovery call? It actually depends on _who_ you're selling to.

We analyzed 519,000 recorded discovery calls with AI to understand what drives successful outcomes.

## Discuss 3-4 Problems During Your Discovery Call

The most effective discovery calls uncover between **three and four business problems**.

The most successful salespeople tend to dive deeply into 3-4 customer problems, and then wrap up logistics and next steps at the end.

Why "3-4 customer problems"? Less than that, and you don't have a buyer in enough pain. More than that, and they have too many priorities going on at once to make any headway with you.

## Ask 11-14 Discovery Call Questions

Asking between **11-14 discovery call questions** during the course of the call correlates with the greatest success.

Less than that and your discovery call may not be robust enough. More than that, and it will likely start to feel like an interrogation, rather than a natural conversation.

## A Few Tips on Discovery Call Questions

The _types_ of discovery call questions you ask matter dearly. Asking a generic line of questioning is likely to get you kicked in the teeth.

Your best bet for discovery call success is asking questions about _key business problems or goals_ the customer is trying to solve for.

We found that top sellers most often ask their questions while discussing problem-related topics with their buyers. They ask _fewer_ questions outside of discussing those topics.

Phrase your questions in a way that prompts your buyer to give you a _long_ response. There's a direct correlation between getting your customer to talk uninterrupted for a long time in response to a discovery call question and closed deals.

Here are some ways you can phrase your questions for a long response:

- _"Can you help me understand…"_
- _"Can you walk me through…"_
- _"Talk to me about…"_

## Make Your Discovery Call Conversational

The more your discovery call feels like a natural conversation, the better. Avoid pitching. But also avoid _interrogating_ your buyer with questions.

Top sellers _balance and spread_ their discovery call questions _evenly_ throughout the sales call. "Average" salespeople, by contrast, "frontload" their questions at the beginning of the call. It's as if they're making their way through a to-do list of pre-loaded discovery call questions.

It also turns out that the more back-and-forth dialogue there is, the more likely you'll succeed. We measured "speaker switches per minute," and found there is a strong correlation with discovery call success.

In other words, your discovery calls should feel like a "tennis match," not a football game.

## The Ideal Discovery Call Talk-to-Listen Ratio

When you follow all of the above discovery call techniques, you naturally end up with a winning talk-to-listen ratio. You listen more. You get your customer to talk more. And you do so in a natural way — one that doesn't feel like an abrasive interrogation or barrage of discovery questions.

## Discovery When Selling to the C-Suite

One year after we conducted this discovery call analysis, we did another one specific to _C-Suite executives_.

Asking too many discovery call questions _greatly_ harms your sale when selling to the C-Suite.

What can you make of this?

**Discovery calls are best done as research tools with other people in the organization.** By the time you get to the C-Suite, you should know your stuff. Putting a C-Suite executive through the typical discovery call is likely to get you kicked in the teeth.

Assuming you've done your homework by hosting discovery calls with _other people_ in the organization, selling to the C-Suite requires a _new sales strategy._ You have to _bring insights to the table when selling to the C-Suite._ Teach them about key problems that have been sitting undetected under their nose. Or important, untapped opportunities they haven't recognized.
