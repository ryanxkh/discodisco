## What is the Challenger sales method?

Developed by business experts Matthew Dixon and Brent Adamson, the Challenger sales methodology is an approach that teaches sellers to reframe buyer priorities, lead with commercial insight, challenge assumptions early, and guide purchasing groups toward decisions aligned with strategic impact across complex deals.

Using the Challenger selling model, GTM teams can standardize messaging, equip sellers to lead deals, and measure adoption across revenue motions. Modern B2B sales organizations continue to rely on the Challenger method to drive consistency in execution, enable scale across regions, and improve plans.

### The 3 T's of modern challenger selling

The short-and-sweet version of the Challenger sales model is that it helps reps:

- **Teach** buyers something fresh about their market, costs, or growth path so meetings feel useful fast, framing issues leaders already feel while showing implications they rarely connect across teams or planning cycles
- **Tailor** insight to each buyer role and deal moment, shifting language, proof, and framing so leaders hear relevance instantly without generic slides slowing meetings or draining attention spans across large B2B buying groups globally
- **Take control** by guiding buyers through tension and tradeoffs, setting pace and direction so meetings advance with purpose, authority, and mutual respect rather than reactive back and forth across stakeholders

"If you teach without tailoring, you come off as irrelevant," Dixon and Adamson wrote in their book. "If you tailor but don't teach, you risk sounding like every other supplier. If you take control but offer no value, you risk being simply annoying."

All three facets of the Challenger sales methodology are dependent on one another and essential to steering the customer conversation toward the optimal, desired resolution: deciding to move forward with your products or services.

### Challenger sales choreography steps

| Step | What this sales pitch step involves |
| --- | --- |
| **The warm-up:** Getting insight into the buyer's world and what matters to them | Start by demonstrating strong knowledge of their business, industry shifts, and challenges tied to strategic growth, team performance, or market pressure. |
| **The reframe:** Introducing a new vantage point to potential customers | Shift focus away from what they expected to discuss and guide them toward a bigger issue or untapped opportunity they haven't fully explored. |
| **The 'rational drowning':** Teaching prospects why inaction is a big problem | Lay out the stakes using credible data and logical arguments to show how the status quo creates unnecessary strain on goals and business impact. |
| **The emotional impact:** Making the challenge personal to your buyers | Show what's at stake for their team, their role, and their brand if things stay the same and how much better life looks with a smarter approach. |
| **The new way forward:** Presenting a path to improving business operations | Help buyers picture a smarter, faster, or more scalable approach that aligns with company goals while reducing complexity across teams and tools. |
| **The present solution:** Explaining why your offering is the one to choose | Tie your capabilities directly to the new path you just outlined, showing how your approach delivers against both strategic and day-to-day needs. |

## Why GTM teams continue to use the Challenger sales model

### Helps reps come prepared to deals with commercial insights tied to leads' business goals

A Challenger sales rep steps into meetings equipped with commercial context that maps to each lead's business goals. They connect economic drivers to specific business opportunities in ways that B2B buying committee stakeholders notice fast.

Insights feel relevant, grounded, and actionable. Nothing feels canned.

When sellers show they understand both the priorities and the pressures, they create space for a serious sales engagement. Buyers stay receptive because the conversation feels familiar but somehow fresher than everything else.

### Empowers sellers to shift the conversation by offering new perspectives to buyers

A Challenger rep earns attention by seeing what others overlook.

More specifically, they show buyers something they haven't fully considered, then guide them to a smarter way of thinking about the same problem.

Sellers who reframe discussions in these moments separate themselves from the pack. They don't mimic competitors. They reset the tone completely.

Buyers leave those meetings with a new way to approach their issues and, more often than not, gain a new appreciation for the rep who helped shape it.

### Assists SDRs in their efforts to relay the emotional implications of making a change

Some sales messaging hits harder than others with leads. Challenger helps reps tap into what's at stake personally and professionally for buyers, whether that's fear of falling behind, pressure from leadership, or worry about wasting budget.

When sellers can touch on the emotional impact of making a change to a partner or vendor tied to prospect's pain points, they shift the conversation.

That shift creates gravity.

Emotion sells because it sticks in ways facts alone never will.

### Gives B2B sales professionals the chance to position their company as a trusted advisor

With the Challenger sales model, members of their sales force transition from product experts into true strategic partners. Reps ask better questions, propose stronger ideas, and tie everything back to outcomes that matter.

That earns trust—fast.

Buyers want someone who knows their business, not just their needs. Sellers who step into this role consistently position their organization as a trusted advisor that is committed to long-term impact beyond a single quarter or contract.

### Ensures sales teams differentiate in crowded markets without relying on discounts

In competitive markets, every SDR (and account executive) needs an edge.

The Challenger approach helps enterprise reps deliver distinct value that doesn't revolve around pricing gymnastics. They frame each problem facing a lead with authority, challenge old thinking, and connect every insight to economic upside.

That turns transactional sellers into reps that buyers listen to and trust.

The sales professionals who master this become self-motivated storytellers who deliver memorable sales pitches that make the case for change. What's more, they make buyers see the positive future in which the decision feels easy.

## Challenger sales model vs. other sales methodologies

Each sales model exists for a reason. They all work beautifully in specific environments, deal dynamics, or team structures tied to how your buyers operate.

Some lean on trust, others on speed or empathy, and a few rely heavily on frameworks that steer reps toward highly detailed discovery or technical depth.

For instance, there's:

- **SPIN selling:** Uses smart, layered questions to uncover high-priority needs
- **SNAP sales:** Simplifies complex choices to help buyers decide faster
- **Sandler Sales:** Builds peer-level trust through mutual accountability
- **Solution selling:** Matches offerings to specific problems buyers articulate
- **Value selling:** Connects offerings to goals that drive business growth
- **Consultative selling:** Guides buyers with insight throughout the entire deal

Each approach rewards different seller instincts, strengths, and sales DNA. Some play to structure, others to adaptability, storytelling, or pure stamina. The challenge isn't finding a perfect system. Rather, it's knowing which one aligns with how your buyers buy and how your reps tend to sell under pressure.

It all comes down to the kinds of sales reps you have on your team (or the kind of mindset you prefer they have when engaging B2B buyers). There's:

- **The hard worker**, who consistently puts in extra effort, follows up relentlessly, and sticks to process without needing reminders, rewards, or hand-holding from leadership.
- **The relationship-builder**, who wins trust early, focuses on rapport over pressure, and plays the long game by connecting on shared values, priorities, and outcomes.
- **The lone wolf**, who works independently, resists structure, trusts their instincts above all, and often delivers based on talent rather than repeatable process.
- **The problem-solver**, who jumps in fast, loves untangling complexity, and aims to be the go-to person buyers call when plans shift or priorities suddenly change.
- **The challenger seller**, who teaches something new, pushes buyers to rethink priorities, and leads with insight that ties directly to strategic and financial upside.

No method wins every deal. That said, certain models sync better with fast deal cycles, long committees, or industries driven by timing and macro change.

Some sales teams blend playbooks, while others commit fully to one approach. It all depends on how you want reps to show up in high-value, high-stakes deals and the specific sales strategy you implement for a given fiscal year.

### Pros and cons of Challenger sales methodology

| Pros | Cons |
| --- | --- |
| Equips reps to lead with insight instead of waiting for buyers to name the exact problem | Requires sellers to bring strong business acumen and fluency in market dynamics early |
| Helps teams frame urgency around business gaps others overlook in their sales approach | Demands regular coaching and oversight from managers to keep usage sharp over time |
| Gives leaders a repeatable model to scale strategic messaging across teams and regions | Adds pressure to sales managers during SDR onboarding and enablement planning phases |
| Creates space for high-impact learning during deals, not just during onboarding or training | Can create pushback from buyers who prefer collaborative, consultative selling dynamics |
| Makes deals less reactive by steering the buyer toward a clearly defined decision path | Needs clear internal alignment so marketing and reps speak to the same commercial themes |
