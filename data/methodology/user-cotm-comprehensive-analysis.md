## 1. Introduction to 'Command of the Message'

### Definition and Primary Objectives

Command of the Message (CoM) is a sales methodology developed by Force Management that focuses on equipping sales teams to effectively articulate value and differentiation when engaging with prospects and customers. The methodology is formally defined as:

"...being audible-ready to define your solutions to customer problems in a way that differentiates you from your competitors and allows you to charge a premium for your products and services."

The primary objectives of Command of the Message include:

- Aligning the entire organization around consistent, value-based messaging
- Enabling sales representatives to communicate value rather than just features
- Providing a framework that helps salespeople adapt to customers' specific needs while maintaining a consistent message
- Positioning products/services as premium solutions by clearly articulating their unique value
- Creating a consultative selling environment where sales representatives serve as trusted advisors
- Improving qualification processes to focus on high-value opportunities

### Origins and Key Principles

Command of the Message was developed by Force Management, a sales consultancy founded by Grant Wilson and David Kaplan. The methodology emerged from their extensive experience in enterprise sales environments, particularly in technology and B2B sectors.

The key principles underlying Command of the Message include:

1. **Value-based selling**: Focusing on business outcomes and customer value rather than product features
2. **Being "audible-ready"**: Preparing sales teams to adapt their approach based on what customers communicate about their business needs
3. **Problem-focused discovery**: Understanding customer problems before proposing solutions
4. **Differentiation**: Clearly articulating what sets your offering apart from competitors
5. **Cross-functional alignment**: Creating consistency across marketing, product, and sales teams
6. **Value capture**: Enabling sales teams to charge premium prices by demonstrating clear value

At its core, Command of the Message recognizes that successful sales organizations must be prepared to quickly and effectively respond to customer needs as they arise during sales conversations, similar to how quarterbacks call audibles during football games. This requires extensive preparation and practice, not improvisation.

## 2. Core Components

### Value Messaging Framework (VMF)

The Value Messaging Framework (VMF) is the central tool of the Command of the Message methodology. This framework provides a structured approach to articulating value and differentiation in a way that resonates with customers. Key elements include:

1. **Value drivers**: The business outcomes and impacts that matter most to customers
2. **Business challenges**: The specific problems customers face that your solution addresses
3. **Solution capabilities**: How your offering solves those problems
4. **Proof points**: Evidence that demonstrates your ability to deliver results (case studies, testimonials, etc.)
5. **Competitive differentiation**: Clear articulation of what makes your solution unique

The VMF is not a script but a dynamic framework that guides customer conversations across multiple channels and touchpoints. It's designed to be "sales-consumable," meaning it's phrased in a way that salespeople can easily use in real-world conversations.

### Value Comprehension, Value Offering, Value Engagement

Command of the Message organizes messaging alignment into three broad categories:

1. **Value Comprehension**: A company's ability to identify and respond to market trends and craft messages that resonate with a broad audience. This is primarily marketing's domain.

2. **Value Offering**: Translating market understanding into product and service offerings, and crafting product-specific messages that address market pains. This is primarily the product team's focus.

3. **Value Engagement**: The ability to articulate value and differentiation at the moment of truth—during sales conversations—and then deliver on that promise. This is where Command of the Message primarily operates.

### The Nine-Step Selling Process

The Command of the Message sales method includes a nine-step process that builds a comprehensive value proposition:

1. **Current situation**: Understanding the customer's current setup, tools, and challenges
2. **Pain points**: Identifying specific problems and challenges
3. **Impact of pain**: Quantifying how these challenges affect the business
4. **Desired state**: Defining what success looks like for the customer
5. **Business impact**: Calculating the value of moving from current to desired state
6. **Solution alignment**: Demonstrating how your solution addresses the specific pain points
7. **Proof points**: Providing evidence through case studies and testimonials
8. **Differentiation**: Articulating why your solution is superior to alternatives
9. **Define next steps**: Creating clear action plans

This process ensures sales representatives thoroughly understand customer needs before proposing solutions, allowing them to position offerings as strategic investments rather than expenses.

### Qualification and Prioritization

Command of the Message helps sales teams better qualify opportunities by focusing on:

- The severity and urgency of customer pain points
- The customer's ability to articulate these pain points
- The presence of quantifiable business impact
- Access to decision-makers
- Competitive positioning

By improving qualification, sales teams can focus their resources on the opportunities most likely to close at premium pricing.

## 3. Implementation Strategies

### Cross-Functional Alignment

Successful implementation of Command of the Message requires alignment across the entire organization:

1. **Executive sponsorship**: Securing leadership buy-in and active participation
2. **Cross-functional involvement**: Including marketing, product, sales, customer success, and other teams in the process
3. **Consistent training**: Ensuring all customer-facing roles understand and use the framework
4. **Shared language**: Developing common terminology and definitions
5. **Aligned content**: Creating marketing materials, sales tools, and other assets that reinforce the value message

### Training and Enablement

Effective training involves several key steps:

1. **Discovery phase**: Understanding the organization's current messaging, products, and market position
2. **Customization**: Adapting the framework to the specific company, products, and industry
3. **Workshop delivery**: Interactive training sessions for sales teams and other stakeholders
4. **Role-playing**: Practicing real-world application through scenario-based exercises
5. **Manager enablement**: Equipping sales managers to coach and reinforce the methodology
6. **Certification**: Ensuring consistent application by certifying trainers and participants

### Integration with Sales Process and Tools

To drive adoption, Command of the Message must be integrated into existing workflows:

1. **CRM integration**: Updating CRM fields to reflect the methodology's components
2. **Sales process alignment**: Embedding the framework in each stage of the sales process
3. **Content management**: Organizing supporting materials for easy access
4. **Coaching tools**: Providing managers with frameworks for reviews and coaching
5. **Playbooks**: Developing scenario-specific guides for common situations

### Reinforcement and Adoption

Long-term success depends on ongoing reinforcement:

1. **Regular reviews**: Making the methodology part of pipeline, opportunity, and deal reviews
2. **Success recognition**: Celebrating wins that demonstrate effective use of the framework
3. **Leadership modeling**: Having executives use the language and approach consistently
4. **Continuous training**: Providing refreshers and advanced training over time
5. **Feedback loops**: Gathering and incorporating user feedback to improve the framework

Force Management offers ongoing support through their Ascender platform, which provides 24/7 access to content, tools, and resources. This helps ensure the methodology becomes "the way we do business" rather than just a training event.

## 5. Comparative Analysis

### Command of the Message vs. MEDDIC/MEDDPICC

**MEDDIC** (Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion) is a qualification-focused methodology that helps sales teams assess and prioritize opportunities.

**Similarities**:
- Both methodologies focus on understanding customer pain points
- Both emphasize identifying decision-makers and processes
- Both can be used in complex B2B sales environments

**Differences**:
- MEDDIC primarily focuses on opportunity qualification, while Command of the Message addresses the entire sales conversation
- MEDDIC is more of a checklist approach, while Command of the Message provides a comprehensive messaging framework
- Command of the Message places stronger emphasis on cross-functional alignment and value articulation
- MEDDIC can be complementary to Command of the Message and is sometimes used alongside it, as seen in the Sysdig case study

### Command of the Message vs. Solution Selling

**Solution Selling** is based on understanding a prospect's needs and recommending solutions tailored to their specific challenges.

**Similarities**:
- Both methodologies are consultative and problem-focused
- Both position the sales representative as a trusted advisor
- Both emphasize understanding customer needs before proposing solutions

**Differences**:
- Solution Selling is more process-oriented, while Command of the Message is more framework-oriented
- Command of the Message places greater emphasis on consistent messaging across the organization
- Command of the Message more explicitly focuses on differentiation and premium pricing
- Solution Selling has a longer history in the market and may have more widespread adoption

### Command of the Message vs. SPIN Selling

**SPIN Selling** (Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff) focuses on a questioning technique that helps sales representatives uncover and develop customer needs.

**Similarities**:
- Both methodologies use structured approaches to understanding customer problems
- Both avoid leading with product pitches in favor of needs-based conversations
- Both aim to build value through consultative approaches

**Differences**:
- SPIN Selling is primarily a questioning methodology, while Command of the Message is a comprehensive messaging framework
- SPIN Selling focuses more on individual sales techniques, while Command of the Message emphasizes organizational alignment
- Command of the Message more explicitly addresses competitive differentiation
- SPIN Selling has a stronger research foundation from its origins

### Command of the Message vs. The Challenger Sale

**The Challenger Sale** promotes a model where salespeople take control of the conversation, teach their prospects, and challenge their thinking.

**Similarities**:
- Both methodologies move beyond traditional relationship selling
- Both emphasize the importance of understanding customer business issues
- Both focus on creating distinctive sales experiences

**Differences**:
- The Challenger Sale is more disruptive in its approach, while Command of the Message is more consultative
- The Challenger Sale emphasizes teaching customers new perspectives, while Command of the Message focuses on aligning with customer-defined needs
- Command of the Message has a stronger emphasis on cross-functional alignment
- The Challenger Sale may be more effective in commoditized markets

## 6. Conclusion and Recommendations

### Key Insights

1. **Value-based approach**: Command of the Message succeeds by shifting conversations from product features to business outcomes and value.

2. **Organizational alignment**: The methodology's emphasis on cross-functional consistency creates a coherent customer experience across all touchpoints.

3. **Framework flexibility**: While providing a structured approach, Command of the Message allows sales representatives to be "audible-ready" and adapt to specific customer situations.

4. **Implementation depth**: Success depends on treating implementation as a change management initiative rather than just a training event.

5. **Measurable impact**: Case studies demonstrate that when properly implemented, Command of the Message can deliver significant improvements in win rates, deal sizes, and revenue.

### Actionable Recommendations for Implementation

For sales teams considering this methodology, these recommendations can help ensure successful implementation:

1. **Secure executive sponsorship**: Ensure leadership actively champions the initiative and models the desired behaviors.

2. **Build cross-functional teams**: Include representatives from marketing, product, sales, customer success, and operations in the development process.

3. **Customize to your context**: Adapt the framework to your specific market, products, and customer base rather than using generic approaches.

4. **Invest in manager enablement**: Equip frontline managers with the tools and skills to coach and reinforce the methodology daily.

5. **Integrate with existing systems**: Embed the framework in your CRM, content management, and other sales tools to drive adoption.

6. **Create feedback mechanisms**: Establish processes to gather input from users and refine the approach over time.

7. **Measure and share results**: Track key metrics before and after implementation, and celebrate successes to build momentum.

8. **Provide ongoing reinforcement**: Treat implementation as a journey, not a destination, with continuous learning and development.

### Final Assessment

Command of the Message offers a comprehensive approach to sales messaging that can transform how organizations engage with customers. Its strength lies in creating organizational alignment around customer value while giving sales teams practical tools to differentiate effectively.

The methodology requires significant investment in time and resources, and success depends on thorough implementation and ongoing reinforcement. However, case studies demonstrate that organizations that make this investment can achieve substantial returns through larger deals, shorter sales cycles, and premium pricing.

For complex B2B sales environments, particularly in technology and services sectors, Command of the Message provides a valuable framework for creating and capturing value through effective sales conversations. Its emphasis on being "audible-ready" with a consistent yet flexible approach makes it particularly well-suited to today's dynamic business environment.
