## **What Is the MEDDPICC Methodology?**

MEDDPICC is a B2B sales qualification methodology used to verify whether a prospect is viable and to map out the steps required to close the deal. It is particularly effective for enterprise sales, but it can be applied in any space with long cycles, high contract values, and multiple stakeholders.

Each letter in the acronym represents a specific criterion that must be validated to eventually close the deal:

- **M** etrics, or the quantifiable economic value a solution provides.
- **E** conomic buyer, or the individual with the ultimate authority to spend the budget.
- **D** ecision criteria, or the technical, vendor, and financial requirements the buyer uses to evaluate a solution.
- **D** ecision process, or the series of steps and approvals the buyer follows to make a decision.
- **P** aper process, or the legal, security, and procurement steps required to finalize the contract.
- **I** dentify pain, or the specific problem, risk, or missed opportunity the prospect needs to solve.
- **C** hampion, or a stakeholder at a prospect organization who advocates for the vendor’s specific solution.
- **C** ompetition, or any alternative solution the buyer is considering (including doing nothing).

Many salespeople have heard of [MEDDIC](https://blog.hubspot.com/sales/a-step-by-step-guide-to-the-meddic-sales-qualification-process?hubs_content=blog.hubspot.com/sales/meddpicc-methodology&hubs_content-cta=meddic), the original framework developed at Parametric Technology Corp (PTC) in the 1990s. The core difference is complexity. MEDDIC (Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion) is excellent for qualifying the opportunity, establishing if there is a fit and a buyer.

MEDDPICC adds two critical components for the modern era:

- Paper Process (P): Acknowledging that getting a “yes” is different from getting a contract signed. In 2025, security reviews and legal redlines can kill a deal just as easily as feature gaps.
- Competition (C): Acknowledging that buyers always have alternatives, even if that alternative is simply sticking with the status quo.

**Pro tip:** MEDDPICC is primarily used by enterprise B2B sales teams selling complex solutions. It’s a good fit for SaaS companies with average deal sizes above $50,000 with sales cycles lasting longer than six months. MEDDIC may suffice for simpler, shorter sales processes; however, even smaller deals may benefit from MEDDPICC's comprehensive approach.

## **MEDDPICC Training and Certification**

While sales leaders can self-teach the basics, formal certification ensures an entire revenue team speaks the same language. Specialized training providers offer deep-dive courses that go beyond definitions into practical application.

### **Why Get Certified?**

- **Standardization:** Leaders ensure every rep qualifies deals using the exact same criteria.
- **Credibility:** A certification signals to employers and buyers that the organization has enterprise-grade sales discipline.

### **Strategic Clarity**

Before a sales methodology can drive revenue, the entire organization must agree on what a “good deal” looks like. MEDDPICC forces leadership and frontline reps to align on the fundamental questions of their sales motion: _What are we selling? Who is the actual buyer? Why does it matter now?_

Through the implementation process, teams must define specific answers for key components:

- **Metrics.** Reps should determine the specific data points that prove value to the prospect.
- **Economic buyer.** Teams need to identify the specific titles and roles with true budget authority.
- **Decision criteria.** Reps should map exactly how the prospect will technically and financially judge the solution.
- **Competition.** Salespeople should acknowledge who else is being considered and how to position themselves against competitors.

This process eliminates ambiguity. Instead of a vague “hope” that a deal will close, the entire revenue engine works from a single source of truth regarding target accounts and winning strategies.

### **Improved Lead Qualification and Team Morale**

One of the fastest ways to burn out high-performing sales reps is to force them to work unqualified leads. When MEDDPICC is fully integrated, it provides a ruthless framework for disqualification. This allows sales professionals to abandon low-probability opportunities early and focus their limited capacity on deals that will actually convert.

This efficiency directly impacts retention and culture. John Rankins highlights this connection in his article, [The Impact of Lead Quality on Your Business](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/impact-lead-quality-your-business-john-rankins-rhmkf/):

“Picture the frustration of your sales team when they continually encounter unqualified leads. It's demotivating to see their hard work result in dead ends. This frustration can lead to burnout, high turnover rates, and a decrease in overall team morale. After all, who wants to make dozens of calls each day, only to be met with rejection or disinterest?”

When teams are empowered to disqualify bad deals, they spend more time winning. This creates a positive feedback loop where success fuels motivation, leading to better sales calls and a superior customer experience.

### **Better Competitive Analysis**

The “Competition” component is a critical addition that separates MEDDPICC from the original MEDDIC framework. It forces organizations to move beyond the naive but, in my experience, surprisingly common assumption that “we have no competition” and confront the reality of the market.

Unless a solution is genuinely the first of its kind in history — a rarity in B2B SaaS — there is always a competitor, even if that competitor is simply the status quo. MEDDPICC demands that sales teams rigorously map their competitors' strengths and weaknesses against their own.

Understanding competitors ensures reps are never blindsided during a negotiation. Salespeople can proactively disarm competitive threats, including inaction, before they derail a deal.

## **How to Implement the MEDDPICC Methodology**

Implementing MEDDPICC transforms how sales teams qualify and advance opportunities. Teams need to rely on metrics and a deep understanding of what the buying team looks like.

Here’s a step-by-step guide for each component of the MEDDPICC process, including what each component is and how to use it to achieve sales success.

### **Metrics**

Metrics are the data or performance indicators that a buyer most needs or values. The job of the sales team is to gather and then effectively communicate the data demonstrating how a product helps buyers achieve desired metrics.

The thing with metrics, as explained by [Joel Popoff](https://www.linkedin.com/in/joelpopoff/), CEO of [Axwell Wallet](https://www.axwellwallet.com/), is that the seller needs to turn data into concrete outcomes that actually mean something.

Popoff says, “Customers connect with concrete outcomes, not abstract data. Rather than emphasizing technical specifications, we offer scenarios like ‘This wallet blocks 99% of RFID skimming threats,’ demonstrating tangible advantages to security-conscious shoppers. This method has considerably increased our conversion rates.”

**Pro tip:** Case studies are helpful in proving a product’s value. [Breeze can help teams create helpful case studies](https://www.hubspot.com/products/content/case-study-generator?hubs_content=blog.hubspot.com/sales/meddpicc-methodology&hubs_content-cta=breeze-can-help-teams-create-helpful-case-studies&hubs_post=blog.hubspot.com/sales/meddpicc-methodology&hubs_post-cta=breeze-can-help-teams-create-helpful-case-studies) to showcase winning metrics that convince prospects to buy.

### **Economic Buyer**

The economic buyer is a key player in the MEDDPICC sales methodology. This person has control of budgets and needs to know that the service will perform for the company. Salespeople may or may not speak with them directly, but it’s vital to understand who this person is and how they can be positively influenced.

For [Tyler Hull](https://www.linkedin.com/in/tyler-hull-5b2238158), founder and sales manager of [Modern Exterior](https://modern-exterior.com/), MEDDPICC’s strength lies in its Metrics _and_ Economic buyer components. Hull believes that these metrics “anchor every discussion on specific, quantifiable results and align them with the decision-maker at the top of the budget.”

Hull explains how this worked: “After introducing MEDDPICC at Modern Exterior, we estimated that our customized solutions reduced clients' project costs by 25% over two years. We’ve noticed prospects progress much further when they are confident in the numbers we provide to support our propositions.”

It makes sense. Decision-makers have skin in the game, and they need to _know_ that their investment will pay off. Data (or outcomes, as Joel Popoff mentions above) gives them that security.

**Pro tip:** Use [Breeze’s Prospecting Agent](https://www.hubspot.com/products/sales/ai-prospecting-agent?hubs_content=blog.hubspot.com/sales/meddpicc-methodology&hubs_content-cta=breezes-prospecting-agent&hubs_post=blog.hubspot.com/sales/meddpicc-methodology&hubs_post-cta=breezes-prospecting-agent) to better understand who’s buying.

### **Decision Criteria**

Decision criteria are the elements that buyers will use to judge a product and vendor. These requirements include things like cost, implementation, collaboration, product quality, and more.

Hull provided insightful examples of decision criteria and how they helped Modern Exterior. He says, “The Decision Criteria component optimized how we sell value, helping us close 15% more deals within six months. In my experience, the framework improves transparency, making it significantly easier to track progress and identify blind spots.”

If a team isn’t sure how its offering is being judged, it’s harder to make proactive changes. One of the best ways to discover decision criteria is to ask.

Buyers want to find the right solution, so they’re likely going to be upfront about what that solution entails. At the same time, some decision criteria could be subconscious or intuitive on the part of the buyer, so go back and review both won and lost deals to determine what was important that might not have been overtly discussed.

### **Decision Process**

The decision process is the “who, what, when” of the selection. It maps the flow from initial interest to the verbal “yes.” Unlike the Paper Process (which is about the contract), this is about the choice.

[Edward White](https://www.linkedin.com/in/edwardjwhiteiii/), head of growth at [beehiiv](https://www.beehiiv.com/), relies on a MEDDPICC sales process. With MEDDPICC, the team at beehiiv discovered an unexpected influencer. White told me: “We found a back-end developer influencing integration decisions, which we’d have missed otherwise. Engaging with them boosted technical alignment and saved us 10+ hours on support post-sale.”

White’s discovery makes complete sense **.** A backend developer should influence integration decisions since it’s likely the developer who would deal with the integration. Any good organization would consult the in-house development resources.

However, in most cases, developers are not in charge of the budget, are absent on sales calls, and therefore might be easy to forget. If the team understands the full decision process, they can spot potential influencers who should be appeased to close a deal quickly.

### **Paper Process**

The “Paper Process” is the second addition to MEDDPICC (from MEDDIC), and it’s often the most neglected step and biggest cause of stalled deals. The Paper Process covers everything that happens after a prospect has chosen a provider but before money changes hands.

Common bottlenecks might include lengthy security questionnaires and reviews, legal redlines over liability caps or data privacy and compliance, or vendor onboarding procedures such as getting set up in a buyer's ERP so that the seller can actually fulfill Purchase Orders.

In a discussion about MEDDPICC, [Jay Soni](https://www.linkedin.com/in/mj-jay-createfabrics/), co-founder and director of sales and marketing at [Yorkshire Fabric Shop](https://yorkshirefabricshop.com/), was quick to mention the paper process.

“Keep detailed documentation of every step of the MEDDPICC process for each transaction. Exhaustive documentation of the ‘Paper Process’ can reveal potential bottlenecks on contract sign-offs or legal audits and allow your team to mitigate them quickly. Believe me, you’ll be reducing risk and creating predictability with this!” Soni says.

### **Identify Pain**

A product or service should solve a pain point for the customer. If the seller knows exactly what that pain point is, they can more effectively show how their solution can alleviate it.

[Jose Angelo](https://www.linkedin.com/in/joseangelo100/), growth marketer and founder of [Jose Angelo Studios](https://joseangelostudios.com/), saw a MEDDPICC implementation increase speed through a sales cycle by 60%.

Angelo says, “The most successful MEDDPICC implementation I have worked on was for a Fortune 500 technology client.” Using MEDDPICC, the client was able to understand who their economic buyer was, the Chief Revenue Officer, and resolve a $2 million revenue leak.

Angelo says, “Structuring the solution directly at their pain points resulted in closing a $400,000 deal within 45 days, or 60% faster than our typical sales cycle.”

### **Champion**

A champion is the person who is most invested in the solution offered by the vendor. They’re already supporting the seller and their business, and they should have some influence over others within their own organization.

Once a champion is identified, the most important thing a seller can do is prepare them with information so they have more ammunition as they advocate for the solution.

Taylor Hull has a tip on getting the most out of a champion. He recommends making the Champion an endpoint, not an extra step.

“It’s essential that your Champion truly understands the value your solution provides in addressing specific pain points and feels empowered to communicate that value internally,” Hull says. “For instance, providing Champions with personalized ROI breakdowns helped us close 25% more deals, as they had the data needed to influence internal stakeholders effectively.”

### **Competition**

Competition is the final “C” in MEDDPICC. Sales teams should always be informed about the competitive landscape, and they should have a keen understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of each member in the market. Ask prospects:

- Who else is involved in the process?
- What other providers are you in discussions with?
- Who are you working with at the moment?
- What do you like/dislike about your current solution?

Finally, don’t forget that there’s always another competitor in the room — the status quo. To reach a prospect that has ignored a problem for years, there’s significant inertia to overcome. Be prepared to articulate why inaction is no longer a viable option.

## **Getting Started with MEDDPICC**

Implementing MEDDPICC requires more than just a spreadsheet. It’s vital to embed it into daily workflows so it becomes muscle memory for reps. To get started, teams need to audit current deals, tidy up their CRM, build qualification systems, and map the selling journey.

Here is a practical roadmap to get started.

Before changing an entire process, run the top 10 active opportunities through the MEDDPICC framework. This exercise creates an immediate educational moment for the team. Don't let MEDDPICC live in notes. Build it into the system of record.

Modern sales tools can do the heavy lifting for reps.

### **4\. Create a Mutual Action Plan (MAP)**

Turn the “Decision Process” and “Paper Process” findings into a shared document with the prospect. A MAP lists every step required to go live — security review, legal sign-off, implementation kickoff, etc. — and assigns owners and due dates. This turns the seller from a vendor into a project manager, guiding the buyer to the finish line.

### **5\. Review deals, don't just inspect them**

Update weekly pipeline reviews. Instead of asking “When will this close?”, ask specific MEDDPICC questions: “Have we tested the Decision Criteria?” or “Has the Economic Buyer confirmed the budget?” If a rep can‘t answer, the deal isn’t qualified.

## **MEDDPICC FAQs**

### **What does MEDDPICC stand for?**

MEDDPICC is an acronym for Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Paper Process, Identify Pain, Champion, and Competition.

### **Is MEDDPICC worth it for my sales team?**

For sellers of high-ticket B2B solutions with sales cycles longer than 3 months, absolutely. Teams using formal methodologies like MEDDPICC can standardize processes and disqualify bad deals earlier.

### **Who owns the MEDDPICC methodology?**

The original concepts of MEDDIC were developed at PTC (Parametric Technology Corporation). While no single entity “owns” the general acronym today, organizations like MEDDIC Academy hold trademarks related to their specific training and certification programs.

### **When should I use MEDDPICC instead of MEDDIC?**

Use MEDDPICC (with the extra P and C) in a highly competitive market or selling to large enterprises where legal/procurement (Paper Process) is a major hurdle. Use MEDDIC with a simpler sales cycle, or one where only a single vendor is usually considered.

### **How long does it take to implement MEDDPICC?**

Full adoption typically takes 3 to 6 months. This includes initial training, configuring a CRM like [HubSpot](https://www.hubspot.com/products/crm?hubs_content=blog.hubspot.com/sales/meddpicc-methodology&hubs_content-cta=hubspot&hubs_post=blog.hubspot.com/sales/meddpicc-methodology&hubs_post-cta=hubspot) to capture additional data, and coaching managers to run deal reviews using the framework.

### **Can MEDDPICC work for smaller deals or teams?**

It can, but it may be overkill for transactional deals under $10 thousand. For smaller deals, a lighter framework like BANT (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline) might be faster. However, the principles of identifying pain and a champion are universal.
